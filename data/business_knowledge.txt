--- MARKET RESEARCH KNOWLEDGE ---

Market research is the process of gathering information about
a target market, customers, and competitors to make informed
business decisions. Key components include:

1. Market Size Analysis: Total addressable market (TAM),
   serviceable addressable market (SAM), and serviceable
   obtainable market (SOM).

2. Customer Segmentation: Dividing potential customers by
   demographics (age, income, location), psychographics
   (lifestyle, values), and behavior (buying patterns).

3. Industry Trends: Analyzing growth rates, emerging
   technologies, regulatory changes, and shifting consumer
   preferences.

4. Demand Analysis: Estimating how many people need your
   product/service and how often they will buy.

For food & beverage businesses:
- The global coffee shop market is worth over $200 billion
- India's cafe market grows at 11% CAGR annually
- Target demographics: students, young professionals, remote workers
- Peak hours: 7-10am and 12-2pm on weekdays
- Average customer spends Rs. 200-500 per visit in India

For retail businesses:
- E-commerce integration is now essential even for physical stores
- Customer loyalty programs increase retention by 30-40%
- Local SEO drives 78% of mobile searches to offline purchases

--- COMPETITOR ANALYSIS KNOWLEDGE ---

Competitor analysis involves identifying direct and indirect
competitors and analyzing their strengths, weaknesses,
opportunities, and threats (SWOT).

Types of competitors:
1. Direct competitors: Same product, same market
2. Indirect competitors: Different product, same need
3. Potential competitors: Not yet competing but could enter

SWOT Framework:
- Strengths: What they do well (quality, price, location)
- Weaknesses: Where they fall short (slow service, high prices)
- Opportunities: Market gaps they haven't filled
- Threats: External factors affecting them

Porter's Five Forces for competitive analysis:
1. Threat of new entrants
2. Bargaining power of suppliers
3. Bargaining power of buyers
4. Threat of substitute products
5. Competitive rivalry

For coffee shop industry:
- Major chains: Starbucks, Cafe Coffee Day, Barista, Blue Tokai
- Independent cafes compete on: ambiance, specialty coffee, loyalty
- Differentiation strategies: specialty brews, food menu, coworking space
- Pricing benchmark: Rs. 150-400 for coffee drinks in urban India

--- FINANCIAL PLANNING KNOWLEDGE ---

A business financial plan includes startup costs, operating
expenses, revenue projections, and break-even analysis.

Startup Costs (one-time):
- Lease deposit: 3-6 months rent upfront
- Interior setup and furniture
- Equipment purchase
- Initial inventory
- Legal and registration fees
- Marketing and branding

Operating Expenses (monthly):
- Rent: 15-20% of expected revenue
- Staff salaries: 25-30% of revenue
- Raw materials/COGS: 30-35% of revenue
- Utilities: 3-5% of revenue
- Marketing: 5-10% of revenue

Key Financial Metrics:
- Gross Margin = (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue
- Break-even point = Fixed Costs / Gross Margin %
- ROI = (Net Profit / Investment) x 100
- Payback period = Total Investment / Monthly Net Profit

For a coffee shop in India (medium size, 500 sq ft):
- Setup cost: Rs. 10-25 lakhs
- Monthly operating cost: Rs. 2-4 lakhs
- Average monthly revenue (stable): Rs. 4-8 lakhs
- Break-even: typically 18-24 months
- Net profit margin: 10-20% after stabilization

--- RISK ANALYSIS KNOWLEDGE ---

Business risk analysis identifies potential threats and
creates mitigation strategies.

Common Risk Categories:
1. Financial Risk: Running out of cash, unexpected costs
2. Market Risk: Low demand, wrong target audience
3. Operational Risk: Staff issues, supply chain problems
4. Regulatory Risk: Licenses, health inspections, compliance
5. Competitive Risk: New competitors, price wars
6. Reputational Risk: Bad reviews, social media crises

Risk Assessment Matrix:
- Likelihood (1-5) x Impact (1-5) = Risk Score
- High (15-25): Immediate action needed
- Medium (8-14): Monitor and plan
- Low (1-7): Accept and watch

Mitigation Strategies:
- Financial: Maintain 3-6 month emergency fund
- Market: Validate idea before investing (pilot testing)
- Operational: Document processes, cross-train staff
- Regulatory: Hire a local consultant for compliance
- Competitive: Build strong brand loyalty

For food businesses specifically:
- FSSAI license is mandatory in India
- Fire NOC and municipal licenses required
- Health inspections can shut business temporarily
- Food safety training for all staff is essential

--- MARKETING STRATEGY KNOWLEDGE ---

A marketing strategy defines how you reach and convert
your target customers into paying, loyal customers.

Marketing Channels:
1. Digital Marketing:
   - Social media (Instagram, Facebook, YouTube)
   - Google My Business for local discovery
   - WhatsApp marketing for repeat customers
   - Email newsletters

2. Local/Offline Marketing:
   - Flyers and posters in nearby areas
   - Partnerships with offices, colleges, gyms
   - Local newspaper and radio ads
   - Events and pop-ups

3. Content Marketing:
   - Food photography for Instagram
   - Behind-the-scenes videos
   - Customer testimonials and reviews
   - Blog posts about specialty coffee/products

Key Marketing Metrics:
- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
- Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)
- Net Promoter Score (NPS)

Marketing Strategy for Food & Beverage:
- Instagram reels showing coffee making = highest engagement
- Google Maps reviews critical — aim for 4.5+ stars
- Loyalty cards increase repeat visits by 35%
- First-visit offers (buy 1 get 1) bring in new customers
- Tie-ups with Zomato/Swiggy for delivery revenue stream

For General Business Launch:
- Pre-launch: Build social media 30 days before opening
- Launch week: Offer discounts, invite local influencers
- Month 1-3: Focus on reviews and word-of-mouth
- Month 4+: Paid ads once you have proof of concept
